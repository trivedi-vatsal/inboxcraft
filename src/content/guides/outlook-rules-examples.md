# 15+ Outlook Rules Examples You Should Be Using

Setting up an efficient inbox requires routing high-volume, low-priority emails out of your main view so you can focus on what actually matters. 

Here are the most common and effective Outlook rule examples you can implement today.

## 1. The "Dev Ops" Funnel
If you are a developer, your inbox is likely crushed by automated system alerts.
- **Trigger:** Sender contains `@github.com`, `@gitlab.com`, `jira@company.com`
- **Action:** Move to `Inbox/DevOps`

## 2. The "Receipts" Catcher
Don't let your Uber or AWS receipts clog your primary view. Route them so they're ready during expense reporting time.
- **Trigger:** Sender contains `receipts@`, `billing@` or Subject contains "Invoice"
- **Action:** Move to `Inbox/Receipts`

## 3. The "Newsletters" Reader
Newsletters are great, but terrible mid-sprint.
- **Trigger:** Sender contains `@substack.com` or Subject contains "Weekly update"
- **Action:** Move to `Inbox/Reading List`
